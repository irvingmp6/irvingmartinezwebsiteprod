Hello World.

My name is Irving Martinez. I'm a Software Engineer.

I am passionate about all-things-computer-science. You will most likely find me in a hoodie with a pot of coffee on my desk while my fingers pound away at the keyboard trying to fix a program that suddenly broke. But it wasn't always like this.

I learned programming later in life. I wasn't some prodigy who grew up hacking computers since I was in the womb. No. That was never me. Hell, I didn't even know what directories were back in high school. It wasn't until my freshmen year when I took an introductory course to Java that I discovered programming, and it was love at first compile. But, to still be honest with you, I didn't really take much from that class other than getting really good at writing System.out.print("Hello World") since the professor bailed on us half of the time. Easy “A”, I guess.

Like most of us, I left college with a broken career compass not knowing what to do with my life. While I jumped around customer service jobs, I still practiced during my spare time, spending long nights and weekends, teaching myself everything there was to know about this programming stuff with a determination that I was going to become a real programmer some day.

Years later, after dozens of personal projects, a personal website, and invaluable industry experience, I stand before you an accomplished engineer.
