# Hello World.

# My name is Irving Martinez.

# I learned to code pretty late in life. I wasn't some prodigy who grew up hacking computers since I was in the womb. No. That was never me. Hell, I didn't even know what directories were back in high school. It wasn't until my freshmen year in college when I was forced to take an introductory course to Java in my STEM program that I discovered programming. It was love at first compile. Though, to be honest, I didn't really get much from that class other than getting really good at writing System.out.print("Hello World"). The professor bailed on us half of the time, but it was an easy A so I couldn't complain.

# It wasn't long before the money ran out, and like most of us, I left college with a broken career compass not knowing what to do with my life. I jumped around jobs, ringing out customers from behind a register, but still coding as a hobby. While I wasn't particularly good at it, it made me feel better about not having a degree.

# My obsession with it started to grow, spending long nights and weekends, consuming articles and videos like no other, going into rabbit holes and coming out with a new perspective to life. I was honing my skills because I had a growing determination that I was going to become a "real" programmer some day.

# Years later, after developing dozens of personal projects, running a personal website, and writing high-quality production software, I stand before you an accomplished Software Engineer.

# While these days, you will most likely find me in a hoodie with a pot of highly charged coffee on my desk, my fingers pounding away at the keyboard resuscitating a program back to life, I'm not your run-of-the-mill. I am constantly looking for the next challenge that'll teach me something else about technology and about myself.
